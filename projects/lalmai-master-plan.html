01 · Overview

A master plan built as a spatial decision system.

Lalmai is a newly formed, predominantly rural upazila facing rapid population growth, fragmented settlement expansion and intense pressure on agricultural land.

The project connects field survey, geodatabase construction and suitability modelling so future growth can be directed rather than merely documented.

06 · Sectoral Plans

Four planning decisions shaped by the spatial evidence.

Each sector converts survey findings into a visible spatial response.

06A · Spatial Growth

Absorb 71% population growth without defaulting to sprawl.

The strategy prioritizes densification, regeneration and extension only inside UDPI-designated growth areas.

06B · Water & Environment

Treat the river–canal network as infrastructure, not leftover land.

The plan protects water corridors, improves drainage continuity and addresses the severe shortage of accessible recreation space.

Recorded recreational land by union

Only 1.76 acres were recorded across five unions.

06C · Disaster Resilience

Design for waterlogging and flood exposure before expansion occurs.

Flood class, inundation depth, local waterlogging evidence and community vulnerability inform the proposed resilience framework.

  • Keep growth away from persistent inundation zones.
  • Restore blocked drainage paths and water connections.
  • Prioritize vulnerable settlements in emergency planning.
06D · Transport

Upgrade connectivity where poor roads isolate people from services.

Road condition, network hierarchy and travel-time assessment identify the links with the greatest service-access impact.

06E · Socio-Economic Profile

Income, livelihood and economic structure

Socio-economic surveys across all 9 unions revealed a predominantly agriculture-dependent economy with emerging service and trade sectors. These findings directly shaped the Economic Development strategy in the Structure Plan.

06F · Urban Services

Education, recreation and waste — a services gap

Field surveys and service-area analysis exposed critical facility deficits: primary schools serve only portions of their intended catchments, playground access is near-zero, and solid waste disposal is largely unmanaged.
